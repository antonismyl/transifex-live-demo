Introducing Cohorts: group users the way your team actually thinks.

Priya Shah · Mar 18, 2026 · 6 min read

Cohorts have always been the most-used feature in Lumen, and — quietly — the one that most often fell short. Our original engine was built for teams tracking a few hundred thousand events a day. As customers scaled past tens of millions, a "simple" cohort query could take the better part of a minute to return. This week we're rolling out a rebuilt cohort engine. It's faster, more flexible, and — we think — a much better match for how product teams actually talk about their users.

What changed

The biggest change is conceptual: cohorts in Lumen are now live by default. When you save a cohort, Lumen keeps it up to date in the background. Open it a week later and the numbers are already there — no re-running, no "stale results" banner, no waiting.

Under the hood, that meant rewriting the evaluator to incrementally apply new events as they stream in, instead of scanning every event whenever you asked a question. The result: cohorts with millions of members now update in roughly 200ms per batch, and ad-hoc exploration queries that used to take 40–60 seconds now come back in under five.

A quick example

Here's what the new cohort definition API looks like. If you've used the old /v1/cohorts endpoint, most of this will feel familiar — but windowed and negated predicates are new:

POST /v2/cohorts
{
  "name": "Power users (30d)",
  "definition": {
    "all_of": [
      { "event": "session_started", "count": { "gte": 10 }, "within": "30d" },
      { "event": "report_exported", "count": { "gte": 1 },  "within": "30d" }
    ],
    "none_of": [
      { "event": "support_ticket_created", "within": "7d" }
    ]
  }
}

The cohort above answers a question we've heard from dozens of customers: "Who are the users getting real value, but haven't had to escalate to support recently?" Previously, answering that required a detour through SQL or a data warehouse. Now it's three predicates.

Why it's faster

Three changes did most of the work:

  1. Roaring bitmaps for cohort membership — we can union, intersect, and difference millions of user IDs in microseconds.
  2. A dedicated materialization layer that keeps "hot" cohorts in memory and evicts cold ones gracefully.
  3. Predicate pushdown into our columnar store so the engine never pulls rows it doesn't need.
We'll go deeper on the engineering in a follow-up post — including the graveyard of approaches that didn't work.

Getting started

Cohorts v2 is enabled for all accounts today. Existing cohorts have been migrated automatically; no action needed on your side. If you want to try the new API, grab a token and hit POST /v2/cohorts — the full reference is in the docs.
